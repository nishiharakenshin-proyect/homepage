Twenty-six parts.
Six pairs of hands.

The Meridian 02 begins with a piece of vegetable-tanned leather drawn from a tannery in Himeji that has not changed its method in 113 years. From there it travels north -- to Aomori, where it is cut, lasted by hand, and stitched onto a midsole of pressed cork.

Every pair carries a tag with the cobbler's initials. There are six cobblers in the workshop. There are 240 pairs each season. The arithmetic is intentional.

March 4, 2026 · Aomori, Japan
Yui Nakahara, founder

We do not believe a shoe should be louder than the ground it walks on.

There is a moment, walking a forest path on a wet morning, when the sound of your own footsteps becomes the loudest thing in the world. The sole presses into the moss, the moss presses back, and for a beat the whole valley seems to be listening. We started TERRA because we wanted shoes that would honor that moment -- not interrupt it.

The first pair we ever made was for a friend who had walked a hundred miles in shoes that didn't fit. We promised her the next hundred would be different.

Every shoe in this catalogue is a conversation between a piece of leather, a piece of cork, and a piece of land. We test each model on the terrain it was designed for, and we name it after that place. The Meridian was walked along a coastal trail on the spring equinox. The Scree was carried up a volcanic ridge in the rain.

We make 240 pairs each season. We do not make more. Not because we cannot, but because we do not want to. A small workshop, a slow rhythm, six pairs of hands. We think a shoe knows when it has been hurried. We think you can feel it when you walk.

Y. Nakahara
